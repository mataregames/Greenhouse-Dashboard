1. Tengeru Select Tomatoes


A vigorous plant that produces firm tasty tomatoes. It is a determiinate plant which means that it grows to a fixed size, is normally bushy and all fruits ripen at about the same time usually two weeks.
  • Germination: 7 to 14 days
  • Maturity: 80 to 90 days
  • Sowing depth: 0.5 to 1 cm
  • Ideal conditions: Full sun in spring and summer and 20 to 24 degrees celsius

2. Fordhook Giant Swiss Chard


  • Maturity:50-65 days
  • LongCultivar
  • Sun: min. 6 hours daily
  • Part shade (Warm)
  • Water: Moderate Feeder
  • Light Suitability : Tolerates partial shade and needs summer shade net and 5 to 30 degrees celsius.
  • Mild flavor with thick, tender leaves.

3. Broccoli


  • Plant Type: Biennial, annual, vegetable
  • Size : 18–30 in. tall, 12–24 in. wide
  • Sun Exposure: Full sun
  • Soil Type : Moist, loamy, well-drained
  • Soil pH : Acidic, neutral
  • 4 to 21 degrees celsius

4. Cherry Tomatoes


Provide your tomato seedlings with 15 hours of bright light each day to keep them from getting leggy. You may need to transplant them into larger pots if they grow very quickly. Tomatoes are hungry plants and they need to be fertilized frequently, starting as seedlings. Depending on the mature size of the variety you are growing, you may want to set a cage, ladder or other vertical support in place at planting time. Keep soil evenly moist, fertilize regularly, and you'll be popping ripe cherry tomatoes in your mouth in eight weeks or less. 21 to 29 degrees celsius
